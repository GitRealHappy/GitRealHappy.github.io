Jesse Boyes

Designer, Wood Artist, Journalist, Community Builder

In the heart of the breathtaking Fraser Valley, my artistic journey with Oakmoss began with aspirations of crafting furniture. Yet, the hum of loud machinery led me to a serene escape—carving. Amidst the mountains, rivers, and diverse ecosystems of BC, I found solace in carving landscapes and wildlife on wood, sometimes painted by local talents like Jack Hendsbee, Connor Koewenhoven, and Keaton Sauder. A serendipitous foray into crafting wooden wallets, initially a limited experiment, blossomed into a collection loved by those who discovered them. Currently a limited-edition run of 50 unique wood wallets are available. Beyond the chisels and brushes, Oakmoss became a catalyst for community. Last summer, I joined forces with local artists and the vibrant funk band Turquoiser to coordinate “Turquoiser Under the Stars” at “The Rusty Shovel”, a groovy venue by the Vedder River. It was a unique blend of concert and art gallery, capturing the essence of our creative vision. Check out the video from this event below, and stay tuned for future events in the Fraser Valley. Oakmoss is more than wood art; it’s a fusion of nature, craftsmanship, and the vibrant spirit of the Fraser Valley.
